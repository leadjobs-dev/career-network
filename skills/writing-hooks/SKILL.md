# Writing Hooks

## Overview

A hook is a micro-contract with the reader: it tells them what they'll get, who it's for, and why it's worth their time — all before they decide to keep reading. Clarity beats cleverness every time. If someone has to work to understand your hook, they're already gone.

**Source:** Synthesized from *The Art and Business of Online Writing* by Nicolas Cole, plus Anton's own LinkedIn performance data (668 posts).

---

## The Curiosity Gap — The 3-Question Test

Every strong hook answers all three questions simultaneously:

| Question | What it does | Failure mode |
|---|---|---|
| **What is this about?** | Tells the reader the topic | Too vague: "I want to share some thoughts..." |
| **Who is this for?** | Identifies the specific reader | Too broad: "Anyone who works..." |
| **What's the PROMISE?** | The outcome/insight they'll receive | Too weak: "...which is interesting" |

- 1 of 3 answered = weak hook
- 2 of 3 answered = decent hook
- All 3 answered = strong hook

The Curiosity Gap = creating enough tension between question and answer that the reader *must* keep going — without giving the answer away in the hook itself.

---

## Anatomy of a Hook

Breaking down: **"The 1 Question That Gets Every Single Millennial In Trouble"**

| Part | Role | Example | Key principle |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Opening (words 1–3)** | Sets tone, signals conviction | "The 1" | Numbers convey certainty + imply short read |
| **The What** | Names the carrot | "Question" | Choose the word that stirs most curiosity: reason / way / mistake / truth / habit |
| **The Connector** | Links opening to promise | "That Gets" | Use sparingly — every extra word costs you |
| **The WHO** | Identifies the reader | "Every Single Millennial" | Be specific: age / role / situation / belief |
| **The PROMISE** | Emotional outcome | "In Trouble" | What readers want to gain *or* desperately want to avoid |

**Readers skim beginning and end.** The opening + the PROMISE are what they actually read. Everything in the middle is connective tissue.

---

## The PROMISE Must Be Emotional

Generic promises lose. Emotional, specific promises win.

| Weak | Strong |
|---|---|
| "become successful" | "never have to think about money again" |
| "avoid making mistakes" | "not get fired on your first day" |
| "improve your habits" | "get more done, hit your goals, gain real financial freedom" |

Test: **What does my reader want most? What do they most want to avoid?** Make the PROMISE speak directly to one of those.

## Rate of Revelation — For Opening Lines

The first line's job: pull the reader's eyes down the page before they consciously decide to keep reading. Every sentence must reveal *new* information or advance the story.

**Slow Rate of Revelation (bad):**
> "I walked into the kitchen to grab a glass of water, which I needed before I could continue writing. I stood there for a while, staring inside the refrigerator..."

**High Rate of Revelation (good):**
> "I walked into the kitchen. The glass fell. Glass in my foot. My neighbor knocked — then the door opened and he had an axe."

For stories: **start at the moment of conflict or achievement.** Not the setup. Not the context. The moment.

> "The first time I became a millionaire, I was living in my parents' basement."

If your post "gets better" in paragraph 3 — delete paragraphs 1 and 2. You don't need them.

## Proven Hook Formats

Use these as starting templates, then make them yours:

| Format | Example | Why it works |
|---|---|---|
| **X Number** | "9 Habits That Will..." | Low barrier to entry, sets expectation |
| **Big Numbers** | "3,000 people just lost their jobs in this city. Here's why." | Concrete + unexpectedly large = exciting |
| **Dollar Signs** | "$400M is how much you need for this house" | Money is universal |
| **Credible Name** | "Will Smith's advice on X will change how you see Y" | Borrowed credibility |
| **This Just Happened** | "Amazon just made a decision that will affect every engineer" | Timeliness = urgency |
| **Success Story** | "How this team closed a $500K deal in 4 days" | Rare outcome = curiosity |
| **Shouldn't Go Together** | "What KFC and Miley Cyrus have in common" | Surprise = scroll-stop |
| **For The Industry** | "3 things every engineering manager gets wrong in year 1" | Specific audience = strong pull |
| **Topic Within Topic** | "7 ways the industry is changing (and how to position yourself)" | Parenthetical adds inside-scoop feel |
| **Question/Answer** | "Can't ship fast enough? Here's the one process change that fixed it" | Good for complex concepts |

**Combine formats for stronger hooks:**
> "The 1 Thing LeBron James Does Every Morning That Earns Him An Extra $10M Per Year"
= X Number + Credible Name + Dollar Signs + Success Story

## POWER Phrases

Add these to any format to increase urgency and signal differentiation:

- "Little-Known" — signals insider info
- "Counterintuitive" — signals surprise
- "Most People Don't Know" — signals exclusive insight
- "After X Years" — signals experience
- "Nobody Talks About" — signals taboo or overlooked angle
- "The Real Reason" — signals revelation vs. conventional wisdom

## Audience Calibration

| Goal | Approach |
|---|---|
| Reach wide audiences | Use universal language, bigger questions (life, money, career) |
| Reach niche audiences | Use ultra-specific language, role/industry callouts |
| Bridge niche → wide | Connect specific insight to universal desire: "How writing code every day taught me to think more clearly about anything" |

## The Rewriting Process (BuzzFeed Rule: 30 versions)

The first hook you write is almost never the right one. Process:

1. **Write it out in full** — don't worry about length
   > "The 8 Things You Should Do On A Daily Basis In Order To Become The Best Person You Can Possibly Become"

2. **Sharpen the PROMISE** — what does the reader actually want emotionally?
   > "...To Get More Done, Achieve Your Goals, And Start Living Your Best Life"

3. **Make vague words specific** — replace abstract nouns with concrete outcomes
   > "...To Be More Productive, Achieve Success, And Gain True Financial Freedom"

4. **Cut connective words** — remove: if, when, does, it, too, for, in order to, that will
   > "8 Daily Habits That Make You More Productive, Hit Your Goals, And Build Real Financial Freedom"

5. **Score against the 3-question test** — if it doesn't hit all 3, rewrite

## Hook Evaluation Rubric

Score 1–10 by answering these questions:

| Check | Weight |
|---|---|
| Does it answer all 3 Curiosity Gap questions? | High |
| Is the PROMISE specific and emotional (not generic)? | High |
| Is the opening (first 2–3 words) strong? | Medium |
| Is the Rate of Revelation fast (no slow setup)? | Medium |
| Does it use or build on a proven format? | Medium |
| Could it be more specific? (Always ask this) | Medium |
| Is clarity prioritized over cleverness? | High |

**Common failure patterns:**
- "I want to talk about X today" → announces topic instead of earning attention
- "Many people struggle with X" → too vague, no WHO
- "Here's something interesting..." → PROMISE is empty
- Slow first sentence that describes context/setup → delete it
- "You should consider doing X" → weak conviction

---

## Quick Diagnostic

Given a hook, ask:
1. What is it about? (Can you answer in 3 words?)
2. Who exactly is it for? (Can you name the person?)
3. What specifically will they get or avoid?
4. Does the first sentence pull you forward — or does it need setup first?
5. What's the strongest word/phrase? Is it at the beginning or end?
6. Could any word be replaced with something more specific or emotional?
